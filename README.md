# Corporate Transactions Analysis - Saasu ERP

## Overview
This project analyses corporate financial transactions using Saasu, 
a cloud-based accounting software. A virtual manufacturing company 
producing the "Beast" was set up in Saasu, and all transactions were 
posted and reviewed at year end. The report covers the year-end 
dashboard, trial balance, accounts payable, accounts receivable and 
inventory management.

---

## What Was Done

### 1. Saasu Dashboard
The year-end Saasu dashboard was reviewed, providing a comprehensive 
summary of the company's financial position including profit and loss 
statements, balance sheet and cash flow analysis. The dashboard 
confirmed all transactions were completed using cash on hand, ensuring 
accurate and up-to-date financial records.

---

### 2. Trial Balance
A trial balance was produced at year end to verify that total debits 
equal total credits across all ledger accounts. Four key unusual 
balances were identified and flagged for management attention:

- **Subscription** — A $1,320 (gross) 12-month subscription to Classy 
  Chassis Monthly ran from July to June. The trial balance only 
  reflects the portion up to 31 December, meaning the remaining 
  subscription period must be correctly allocated as a prepayment.

- **Rent** — $12,000 (net) was paid in advance for a 12-month lease 
  running from September to August. Only the rent to 31 December is 
  shown in the trial balance, requiring proper allocation of the 
  remaining prepaid rent into the following year.

- **Tax Collected** — All sales and purchase taxes must be tracked and 
  paid on time. Delays or inconsistencies in tax payments could result 
  in fines, compliance issues or operational risk.

- **Cash on Hand** — The year-end cash balance was just $448.80, a 
  very low figure that requires strategic reinvestment in manufacturing 
  and strict cost control to support business continuity.

---

### 3. Accounts Payable
At year end, the company owed a total of $20,130 AUD to suppliers, 
including Syrup Property Co ($13,200 AUD) and Itzall Sheet Products 
($3,960 AUD). Key points covered:

- Accounts payable represents amounts owed to suppliers for goods and 
  services purchased on credit, typically due within 30 to 60 days
- A healthy AP balance is important — a higher AP turnover ratio 
  indicates the business is generating sufficient revenue to meet 
  short-term obligations and gives stronger leverage when negotiating 
  terms with suppliers
- Challenges identified include slow manual processing, payment errors, 
  inconsistencies in invoice matching and increased fraud risk from 
  manual intervention

---

### 4. Accounts Receivable
At year end, two outstanding client balances were recorded totalling 
$11,066 AUD — Toyota [Barfson, Jeremy] with $11,000 AUD and Stagg, 
John with $66 AUD. Key points covered:

- Accounts receivable represents funds owed to the company for goods 
  or services delivered but not yet paid
- Management aims to keep AR low because a shorter receivables period 
  improves the cash conversion cycle, increases liquidity and gives 
  the business more flexibility to reinvest profits
- A high Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) indicates payment delays that 
  reduce liquidity and strain cash flow
- Challenges include slow payment cycles, manual reconciliation errors 
  and ineffective credit policies

---

### 5. Inventory
The inventory system for the "Beast" manufacturing company was set up 
using two categories in Saasu:

- **Items** — individual components purchased and/or sold, showing 
  on-hand quantity, average cost and selling price
- **Combo Items** — finished products built from separate inventory 
  items, treated as a separate warehouse from the items inventory

Management actions recommended for inventory:
- Review stock levels and evaluate slow-moving items
- Assess quality and obsolescence risks
- Review pricing strategy and consider promotional campaigns

**Competitor Pricing Impact:** If a competitor begins selling an 
identical product for $175 while the current selling price is $500, 
this could significantly reduce sales and market share. Customers 
may switch to the lower-priced competitor unless the product can be 
differentiated through improved quality, additional features or better 
customer service. A pricing strategy review would be required, 
potentially including price adjustments, cost reduction through 
improved efficiency, and reinforcing customer loyalty.

---

## Key Takeaway
This project demonstrates how ERP software such as Saasu can be used 
to record, track and review all corporate financial transactions. The 
year-end analysis highlighted key risks including low cash reserves, 
prepayment misallocations, outstanding receivables and inventory 
pricing pressure from competitors. Accurate financial reporting through 
tools like Saasu supports informed decision-making for management and 
shareholders alike.
